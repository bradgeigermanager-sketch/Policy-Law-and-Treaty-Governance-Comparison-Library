A governance operating system—with clear layers, engines, and artifacts that all interlock.


---

1. Conceptual layers

- Instruments:
  - Treaties: treaty., treaty_version.
  - Laws: law.federal., law.state., law.local., law_version.
  - Policies: policy.org., policy_version.
- Scopes:
  - Jurisdictions: jurisdiction., jurisdiction_profile.
  - Parties: party., party_profile.
  - Participants: entity.*
- Targets / Affected things:
  - impact_target.* (rights, activities, resources, processes, state variables)

---

2. Core analysis engines (per layer)

Treaties

- Treaty overlap & compatibility (pairwise + multilateral)
- Cross‑Treaty Optimization Engine: crosstreatyoptimization_engine
- Treaty Harmonization Engine (implied by symmetry with law)
- Participant‑Level Treaty Impact Simulator: participanttreatyimpact_simulator

Laws

- Participant‑Level Law Impact Simulator: participantlawimpact_simulator
- Cross‑Law Optimization Engine: crosslawoptimization_engine
- Law Harmonization Engine: lawharmonizationengine

Policies

- Policy Impact Engine: policyimpactengine
- Policy group impact simulator: policygroupimpact_simulator
- (Org policy harmonization is structurally implied and pluggable)

---

3. Cross‑layer & interaction engines

- Law–Treaty Interaction Engine: lawtreatyinteraction_engine
  - Maps treaty. ↔ law. interactions: conflicts, reinforcements, gaps.
- Cross‑Layer Impact Aggregation Engine: crosslayerimpactaggregationengine
  - Aggregates treaty/law/policy impact reports into report.crosslayerparticipant_impact.*.
- Unified Participant Impact Engine: unifiedparticipantimpact_engine
  - Single‑pass computation of:
    - impact.unified_obligation.*
    - impact.unified_right.*
    - impact.unified_limitation.*
    - conflict.unified_participant.*
    - impact.unified_risk.*
    - report.unifiedparticipantimpact.*

---

4. Impact & state modeling

- Per‑layer impacts:
  - Treaty: impact.obligation., impact.limitation., conflict.treaty_participant., impact.risk.
  - Law: impact.lawobligation., impact.lawright., impact.lawlimitation., conflict.lawparticipant., impact.law_risk.*
  - Policy: impact.policyobligation., impact.policylimitation., conflict.policyparticipant., impact.policyrisk.
- Cross‑layer / unified:
  - report.crosslayerparticipant_impact.*
  - conflict.crosslayerparticipant., impact.crosslayerrisk.
  - impact.unified., conflict.unifiedparticipant., impact.unified_risk.
- Multi‑layer impacted state:
  - impactstate.multilayer.*:
    - For each impacttarget.*, list layer‑specific effects and compute combinedeffect.net_status (allowed / prohibited / required / ambiguous / conflicting).

---

5. Optimization & harmonization

- Cross‑Treaty Optimization Engine: crosstreatyoptimization_engine
  - Optimizes treaty configurations via opt.treatyconfig., opt.treatymetrics., opt.treatyglobalobjective.*.
- Cross‑Law Optimization Engine: crosslawoptimization_engine
  - Optimizes domestic law configurations via opt.lawconfig., opt.lawmetrics., opt.lawglobalobjective.*.
- Law Harmonization Engine: lawharmonizationengine
  - Proposes law.harmonization_change.* to reduce conflicts and improve compatibility.
- (Policy and treaty harmonization engines are structurally parallel and can reuse the same patterns.)

These engines sit above the impact engines and use them as evaluation oracles.

---

6. Time, versions, and DAG

- Versioned instruments:
  - treatyversion., lawversion., policy_version.*
- Time‑sliced unified impact:
  - Engine: unifiedparticipantimpacttimesliceengine
  - Output: report.unifiedparticipantimpact_timeslice.* (impact “as of” a given datetime).
- Version‑aware governance DAG: dag.governanceversionaware.*
  - Nodes:
    - node.instrument.*
    - node.instrument_version.*
    - node.jurisdictionorparty_scope.*
    - node.participant_scope.*
    - node.impact_timeslice.*
  - Edges:
    - edge.instrumenttoversion.*
    - edge.versiontoscope.*
    - edge.scopetoparticipant.*
    - edge.participanttoimpact.*
  - Gives full traceability: which version of which instrument produced which impact at time T.

---

7. Simulation, scenarios, and risk surfaces

- Governance Simulation Engine: governancesimulationengine
  - Applies scenario.governance. to state.governance. and recomputes unified impacts + heatmaps.
- Governance Scenario Generator: governancescenariogenerator
  - Generates candidate scenarios to reduce conflicts/risk or test new instruments.
- Global Risk Heatmap Engine: globalriskheatmap_engine
  - Produces heatmap.governance_risk.* across dimensions:
    - participant, jurisdiction, layer, instrument_type.

---

8. UI schemas

- Unified Governance Impact Dashboard: ui.governanceimpactdashboard.*
  - Summary header, participant table, layer overview, risk hotspots, filters/scenarios.
- Participant‑Level Law Impact Panel: ui.lawparticipantimpact_panel.*
  - Header, jurisdiction scope, obligations/rights/limitations, conflicts & risks.
- (Analogous panels can be defined for treaty, policy, and unified impact using the same patterns.)

---

9.

In plain terms, this system includes:

- A version‑aware, time‑aware DAG of governance instruments.  
- Layer‑specific impact engines (treaty, law, policy).  
- Cross‑layer aggregation and a unified participant impact engine.  
- Optimization and harmonization engines for treaties and laws.  
- A multi‑layer impact model that can say, for any “thing” (right, activity, resource, process):  
  - how each layer treats it,  
  - what the net effect is,  
  - and how that changes over time or under scenarios.  
- Simulation, scenario generation, and risk heatmapping on top.

The next natural additions are:

- a diff model for comparing two unified impact states (time A vs time B, or scenario vs baseline), or  
- a constraint language for expressing “acceptable governance states” that the optimization engines must satisfy.
